History

History

1979-2023

The history is created by winners

Founded in February 1979 by Professor Antonio José Restrepo with the goal of establishing the first University Scuba Diving Club in Colombia, carrying out activities at the University of Antioquia. Initially, it was named the University of Antioquia Orcas Underwater Activities Club.

In 1989, Luis Fernando Botero was elected as the president of the club, who ventured into the sports of Underwater Rugby and Underwater Hockey. In 1988, Orcas Club organized the first National Championship of Underwater Rugby and Underwater Hockey in Antioquia.

With time, the Hockey team gradually disappeared, while the Rugby team, on the contrary, continued to strengthen and is currently the club's only competitive sports discipline.

Since its beginnings, Underwater Rugby demonstrated itself as a strength and gradually gained recognition on the national level, consistently competing for top positions. The first international participation by club members occurred at the 1991 World Championships in Finland, with three players selected to join the Colombian National Team

The first international participation by club members was in the 1991 World Championships in Finland, with three players selected to join the Colombian National Team.

This is how the Orcas Club increasingly contributed more players to the Colombian national teams that would compete in the World Championships. The club continued to improve its level, and by the 2003 World Championship, it had the core of the Colombian national team.

In addition, the club has served as the foundation for the Antioquia Selection in both the male and female teams since the Interleague tournaments began.

The club continued to win national championships, and in 2010, the Orcas team decided to travel to Berlin, Germany for the first time to participate in the Champions Cup, an international tournament where the champion clubs from each country that practices Underwater Rugby compete. In this tournament, the women's team secured the first place, and the men's team finished in second place. This achievement led to the women's team being awarded the Acord prize for the Best Sports Club in the year 2010.

In this tournament, the women's team achieved first place, and the men's team secured second place. This led to the women's team being honored with the Acord award for the Best Sports Club in 2010

At the end of 2010, the club was displaced from the University of Antioquia and, as a result, started training at the Aquatic Complex managed by INDER (Instituto de Deportes y Recreación de Medellín), which required Orcas Club to go through the necessary procedures to establish itself as a sports club under the administration of INDER Medellín

On March 3, 2011, Orcas Club was re-established under the name CLUB DEPORTIVO ORCAS DE RUGBY SUBACUATICO and began training at the Aquatic Complex of the Stadium, which expanded its training facilities and the number of athletes.

In 2011, Orcas' men's team returned to the Champions Cup, where they secured third place. Starting from this year, both the men's and women's Orcas teams have consistently attended the Champions Cup in Berlin, as they have maintained their leadership at the national level.

Their greatest achievements include winning the Champions Cup in 2019 and 2022 in the men's category and in 2010 in the women's category

They also served as the base club for the Colombian National Teams in 2019 and 2023 (men), achieving the gold medal in the World Championship for adults. Additionally, they were the base club for the Colombian National Team in 2023 (women), which, for the first time, became world champions. Winning this championship, which is held every four years, is the highest achievement in the sport.s

As of today, the club has more than 90 athlete members and over 20 junior athletes. We have remained undefeated as national champions for many years and are a team of national and international champions.
